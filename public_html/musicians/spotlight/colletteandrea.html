Spotlight: COLLETTE ANDREA

 

 

Article and photos by Edith Nataprawira

Uploaded on January 12, 2013
There was a low rumble of conversation in the café when I met Collette Andrea for the first time. Several rounds of email tag had finally paid off, as the high waters that were Andrea’s fourth year social work co-op schedule finally parted and gave us enough time to meet.

The self-taught musician made learning an instrument without formal instruction seem deceptively easy as she strummed her guitar over the din.

Andrea explained that she had taught herself to play bass guitar when she was 12 years-old. At 14, she began to write and perform her own songs, dabbling with different sounds and musical chords.

When asked to describe her own music, Andrea notes that her songs tend to “lean towards the contemplative.” Andrea’s lyrics speak of her own experiences, encounters with strange acquaintances, and even simple daily occurrences.

Listening to artists like Amy Winehouse, Bessie Smith, Billie Holiday, and Alice In Chains, Andrea's acoustic style became a conglomeration of blues, rock and grunge. Being in charge of her own musical education gave Andrea the power to choose and experiment with genres that interested her.

"Right now I'm really inspired by blues music," Andrea says. Andrea also names contemporary Canadian singer Leslie Feist as a source of inspiration, especially because "It's good to see that there are other women out there who can make and control their own music."

When asked whether she would consider a formal education in music, Andrea replies, "Some theory would be helpful but if I took music at school it would be a chore--something I'd have to keep up with." Instead, Andrea is just as dedicated to her current field of study as she is to her music. "I've always wanted to be a social worker," she said, "but I definitely want to take the time when I'm done school to do my music."

Andrea continues to share her music and would like to collaborate with fellow musicians in the future. Andrea's first EP, Luna, is currently available online and her next EP will be released in early 2014, so be sure to keep your eyes peeled for updates and upcoming performances.
